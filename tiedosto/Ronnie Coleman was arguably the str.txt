Ronnie Coleman was arguably the strongest bodybuilder in history, possessing strength comparable to competitive powerlifters. During his prime, he famously performed 800-lb (363 kg) squats and deadlifts for reps, a 2,300-lb leg press, and 200-lb dumbbell presses. His training was defined by extreme, heavy,, and high-volume sets. Verified Key Lifts
Squat: 800 lbs (363 kg) for 2 reps
Deadlift: 800 lbs (363 kg) for 2 reps
Leg Press: 2,300 lbs (1,043 kg) for 8 reps
Bench Press: 500 lbs (227 kg) for 5 reps
Dumbbell Row: 200 lbs (91 kg) per hand for 12 reps 
Strength Characteristics
Powerlifting Focus: Unlike many bodybuilders, Coleman's training regimen was deeply rooted in powerlifting, which allowed him to lift in the 800+ lb range for squats and deadlifts.
High Reps with Massive Weight: He was known for doing sets of 10-12 reps with weights that most people would struggle to lift for one, such as barbell rows with 495 lbs.
