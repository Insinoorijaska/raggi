To increase running stamina, prioritize consistency by running 3–4 times per week, gradually increasing weekly mileage by no more than 10% to prevent injury. Incorporate long, slow, conversational-paced runs to build aerobic capacity, alongside HIIT, tempo runs, and strength training to improve efficiency. 

Key Strategies for Improving Stamina:
Run Long and Slow (Base Training): Focus on endurance by running at a comfortable, conversational pace (roughly 80% of your race speed) for longer durations. This builds aerobic capacity and improves metabolic efficiency.
Gradual Mileage Increase: Follow the "10% rule"—increase your total weekly running distance or time by no more than 10% to allow your body to adapt without injury.
Incorporate Interval Training (HIIT): Alternate between high-intensity sprints and low-intensity rest (or walking) to improve cardiovascular speed and efficiency.
Include Tempo Runs: Run at a "comfortably hard" pace for a sustained period to increase your lactate threshold, allowing you to run faster for longer.
Strength and Cross-Training: Use exercises like squats, lunges, and plyometrics (box jumps) to strengthen leg muscles. Incorporate low-impact cardio like cycling or swimming to build endurance without overtraining.
Consistency is Key: Schedule3–4 runs per week to maintain and improve cardiovascular endurance. 

Supporting Factors for Stamina:
Nutrition: Consume a diet rich in complex carbohydrates and lean protein for sustained energy, and ensure proper hydration before and during runs.
Rest and Recovery: Allow time for recovery to prevent burnout and injury.
Breathing Techniques: Practice deep breathing and proper running form to optimize oxygen consumption. 

For beginners, start by alternating running and walking, or follow a structured plan like a 5k training program.
