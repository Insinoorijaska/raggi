An average, untrained man can typically lift roughly his own body weight in a squat or deadlift, and around 0.5–0.75 times his body weight on the bench press. Common beginner benchmarks include a 135 lb bench press, 135 lb squat, and 225 lb deadlift. With training, these numbers can significantly increase, with an intermediate lifter often reaching a 225 lb bench and a 315 lb+ squat. Strength Benchmarks for the Average Man (Approximate)
Untrained/Beginner:
Bench Press: ~135 lbs (or 
 0.5 - 0.75 x body weight)
Squat: ~135 lbs (or 
 1x body weight)
Deadlift: ~155-225 lbs
Novice (6-12 months training):
Bench Press: ~155-175 lbs
Squat: ~185-225 lbs
Deadlift: ~250-300 lbs
Intermediate (1-2 years training):
Bench Press: ~185-225 lbs
Squat: ~225-275 lbs
Deadlift: ~275-350 lbs
Key Factors & Context
Body Weight: These numbers vary significantly based on the individual's weight; heavier individuals tend to have higher raw strength numbers.
Definitions: "Average" often refers to a man who does not regularly lift weights.
Progression: Within the first year of consistent, proper training, these numbers can double.
Safety: For daily tasks, it is generally recommended that men do not lift more than 25 kg (
 55 lbs) without assistance, especially if lifting away from the body.
